CLAMPS Info

The Collaborative Lower Atmospheric Mobile Profiling System (CLAMPS) combines Doppler lidar, microwave radiometer, and AERI thermodynamic profiling in a transportable trailer platform operated by NOAA/NSSL and the University of Oklahoma.

What is CLAMPS?

CLAMPS was designed to measure profiles of temperature, water vapor, and wind in the lowest several kilometers of the atmosphere at high temporal resolution. The integrated instrument suite supports research on convection initiation, boundary-layer evolution, mesoscale dynamics, wind-energy applications, and operational nowcasting concepts. Two sibling facilities — CLAMPS1 (2015) and CLAMPS2 (2016) — have been deployed across field campaigns throughout the United States and abroad.

For complete facility documentation, instrument history, retrieval methods, and science applications, see the Wagner et al. (2019) BAMS overview paper and the accompanying review manuscript that this site supports.

Instrument suite

  • Doppler lidar — scanning and staring wind profiles, turbulence, and cloud-base detection
  • Microwave radiometer (MWR) — temperature and moisture profiles, including through some cloud layers
  • AERI — high-spectral-resolution infrared radiance for TROPoe thermodynamic retrievals
  • Surface met & radiosondes — supporting surface observations and balloon launches
